# CouchLoop Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

CouchLoop exists to make AI interactions safer, more thoughtful, and more human. We hold ourselves and our community to the same standard. Everyone who participates in this project, whether by writing code, reporting issues, asking questions, or simply using the tools, deserves to be treated with dignity and respect.

## Our Values

### Safety First

We build tools that protect people from harm. That principle extends to how we treat each other. We do not tolerate behavior that makes others feel unsafe, unwelcome, or diminished.

### Honesty Over Ego

CouchLoop was born from the recognition that AI systems can hallucinate, mislead, and cause real damage when left unchecked. We value the same honesty in our human interactions. Say what you mean. Admit when you're wrong. Ask when you don't know. There is no shame in uncertainty, only in pretending it doesn't exist.

### Empathy as a Practice

Our wellness tools exist because we believe reflection and emotional awareness matter. We expect community members to extend that same empathy to one another. Before responding in frustration, pause. Consider the person on the other side of the screen. Assume good intent until proven otherwise.

### No Stupid Questions

If someone asks a question, it means they want to learn. That deserves encouragement, not eye rolls. Whether it's "what does MCP stand for?" or "how do I run the tests?", every question is a sign that someone cares enough to try. The only bad question is the one that goes unasked because someone was afraid of being judged.

### Trust is Earned, Not Given

Trust is hard to earn and easy to lose. In this community, we earn it through consistency: by showing up, doing what we say we'll do, writing code we stand behind, and treating people the way we'd want to be treated. Respect isn't a title or a badge. It's built one interaction at a time, and it takes real work to keep. Cut corners with people's trust and you won't get it back easily.

### Quality Through Collaboration

Great software is built by people who can disagree respectfully, give honest feedback without cruelty, and receive criticism without defensiveness. We encourage robust technical debate. We discourage personal attacks disguised as technical opinions.

## Expected Behavior

- **Be welcoming.** Use language that invites participation from people of all backgrounds and experience levels.
- **Be constructive.** When reviewing code or responding to issues, focus on improvement rather than blame.
- **Be patient.** Not everyone has the same context you do. Take the time to explain rather than dismiss.
- **Be accountable.** If you make a mistake, in code or in conduct, own it, learn from it, and move on.
- **Respect privacy.** Do not share personal information about others without their explicit consent. This is especially important given our project's wellness and mental health dimensions.
- **Prioritize security.** Follow the project's security practices. Never commit secrets, credentials, or personal data. Report vulnerabilities responsibly.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Personal insults, derogatory comments, or deliberate attempts to make someone feel unwelcome
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Sustained disruption of discussions or project activities
- Dismissing or mocking someone's mental health, emotional state, or personal struggles
- Weaponizing project tools or community spaces to cause harm
- Deliberately introducing malicious code or security vulnerabilities

## Mental Health and Wellness

CouchLoop has a wellness component that touches on sensitive topics: reflection, emotional processing, personal growth. This comes with additional responsibility:

- **Do not mock or trivialize** anyone's use of wellness features or their willingness to engage with emotional content.
- **Do not play therapist.** Our tools provide structured reflection, not clinical advice. Community members should not offer unsolicited mental health diagnoses or treatment recommendations.
- **Be mindful of crisis situations.** If someone in the community appears to be in distress, encourage them to reach out to appropriate professional resources rather than attempting to intervene yourself.

## Privacy Standards

CouchLoop handles sensitive data, including session content, wellness reflections, and personal context. Privacy is not a feature; it is a responsibility.

- **Minimize data collection.** Only collect and store what is necessary for the tool to function. If you don't need it, don't ask for it.
- **Never log personal content.** Session data, wellness reflections, and user messages must never appear in application logs, error reports, or analytics.
- **Encrypt at rest and in transit.** All stored user data must be encrypted. All network communication must use TLS.
- **Honor deletion requests.** When a user asks for their data to be removed, it must be fully deleted. Not archived, not soft deleted, not forgotten in a backup.
- **No silent tracking.** Do not add analytics, telemetry, or third party tracking without explicit disclosure and user consent.
- **Credential hygiene.** Never commit API keys, tokens, database connection strings, or any secrets to the repository. Use environment variables exclusively. See the project's security guidelines in [CLAUDE.md](CLAUDE.md) for detailed requirements.
- **Contributor privacy.** Do not share personal details about contributors, such as real names, locations, employers, or contact information, without their explicit permission.
- **Wellness data is sacred.** Data generated through wellness and reflection features is inherently personal. Treat it with the highest standard of care. It must never be used for marketing, shared with third parties, or surfaced in any public facing context.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all CouchLoop project spaces, including GitHub repositories, issue trackers, pull requests, discussion forums, chat channels, and any events or meetups associated with the project. It also applies when representing the project in public spaces.

## Reporting and Enforcement

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct:

1. **Contact the maintainers** at conduct@couchloop.com (or via GitHub by opening a private issue).
2. Include as much detail as possible: what happened, when, where, and any relevant links or screenshots.
3. Reports will be reviewed promptly and handled with discretion.

### Consequences

Maintainers will respond to violations based on severity and context:

- **First occurrence (minor):** A private conversation to clarify expectations.
- **Repeated or moderate violations:** A formal warning with specific required changes in behavior.
- **Severe or persistent violations:** Temporary or permanent removal from project spaces.

Maintainers commit to applying these guidelines consistently and without favoritism.

## A Note on AI and Responsibility

We are building governance tools for AI. That means we think deeply about the consequences of technology. We ask the same of our community: think about the impact of your words and actions. Technology reflects the values of the people who build it. Let's make sure ours are worth reflecting.
